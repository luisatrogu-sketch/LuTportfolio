Project 02
Digital Money Made Simple

Understanding The Problem

Understanding of the context and what was already been done

During our initial conversations, we understood that a lot of data was already available. This proved both the need to improve the digital money transfer service and the strong interest of their customer base in becoming banked by our client. The data was clear. Less evident were the motivations driving customers to stay with our client for banking services - especially for pure digital banking. Many of their customers were unbanked, not digitally savvy, and far from financially savvy. Being migrants, they were deeply rooted in their home countries while also trying to establish themselves in the new destination country.

We therefore proposed to carry on a series of interviews, in which we would validate the data already available and understand the above mentioned motivations that would have helped us to move core customers gradually away from cash, educating them to digital money.

Exploration and validation

We conducted several interviews across three destination countries (and few guerrilla shadowing sessions when lock down rules in Italy relaxed slightly). We collected insights on habits and common struggles with money management, use of digital channels, and relationships with financial institutions. We also looked at customers' feelings and ambitions regarding their sense of belonging to two different countries and cultures.

Customer Interview Insights

Highlights

As a result, customers confirmed their trust in our client as a potential financial institution to serve them as a bank. However, they also showed a strong need for more than just digital interaction; they wanted guidance, human support, and a personal connection.

Solving

Not Only Digital

In this phase of the project, we focused on creating a solution that was both business-viable and aligned with customers' needs and expectations. Although the client had heard the demand for more than just digital interaction and a stronger personal connection with the brand, we still needed to further convince them to go beyond being only a digital bank. We allied with the part of client team more sensitive to the topic, more linked to the field and into more external cases to use as example and inspiration. We managed to at least prepare a plan for a whole experience in which the brand would provide also physical moments of contact to its customers and state this plan in a customer journey.

Testing

Validating the viability and affordability of the proposed solutions

We did two runs of tests for the services we conceived:

  • first one just testing the scenarios on line with the support of sketched storyboards
  • once we had a clearer idea of potential situations, experience and revised services we proposed an in presence test with a simulation of potential uses of digital services, risks and attacks and evaluated the response of interviewees.
